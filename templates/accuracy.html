Accuracy

In our osteoporosis prediction project, we used machine learning to find out whether a person has a high chance of getting weak bones. The model learns from many patient data like age, gender, bone density, calcium level, lifestyle, etc. After training the model, we checked how correctly it can predict osteoporosis. This is called accuracy. Our model showed good accuracy, which means it gives correct results most of the time. A higher accuracy shows that the machine learning model understands the patterns in the data and can help doctors make better and faster decisions. This accurate prediction can help in early detection and early treatment so that people can avoid bone fractures and stay healthy.
