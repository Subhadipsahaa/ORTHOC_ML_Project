Osteoporosis is a widespread condition characterized by reduced bone density, leading to an increased risk of fractures. Early prediction and intervention are crucial for preventing fractures and improving patient outcomes. More sophisticated procedures must be investigated because traditional diagnostic methods, such bone mineral density (BMD) testing, frequently fall short in identifying osteoporosis in its early stages. Using a range of clinical, demographic, and lifestyle characteristics, this research explores the use of machine learning (ML) algorithms to predict osteoporosis risk. Several machine learning models, such as decision trees, support vector machines, random forests, and neural networks, were used to assess data from multiple sources, including age, gender, medical history, physical activity, and eating habits. The main goal was to create a precise, data-driven model for early osteoporosis detection so that prompt actions could be taken to enhance bone health and prevent fractures.
